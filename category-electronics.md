# Electronics & appliances — category standards (fit-first, live-checked)

> **This file is a map, not a rulebook.** Models, specs, prices and certification
> statuses change constantly — this file holds the **decision questions, the axes
> that matter, the marketing traps, and where to fetch evidence**. It does **not**
> name "the best" model, freeze a spec, or assert a current certification status.
> **Fetch the current lineup, specs, tests, prices and cert status live** — even the
> flagship's name is a moving target.
>
> ## Fit first — elicit the use-case before recommending
> For electronics, "good" is almost entirely **use-case-dependent**, so the biggest
> mistake is answering "best X" with a context-free flagship. **Don't do that.**
> - **A bare "best laptop / phone / TV" is not answerable yet.** Ask the **2–3
>   questions that actually fork the pick** (see each sub-category) — as tappable
>   options where possible — then recommend. If I've already given the constraints,
>   **don't re-ask; proceed.** You may offer a provisional lean while asking.
> - **Then be decisive:** return a **2–3 shortlist with a clearly marked #1** for my
>   profile + the one reason it wins, and who each alternate is for. Not a lone
>   dictated pick, not a raw dump.
> - **"Best" always means "best for *my* use + budget."** A flagship is only "best"
>   if it fits the stated need; say so, don't assume it.
> - **Budget** is elicited as a use-case constraint here (electronics almost always
>   has one) — still *budget as a filter*, ranking quality within it, never letting a
>   low price override safety or a genuine quality gap (per *Ranking* in
>   `instructions.md`).
>
> ## What "good" means for electronics (ranked, safety first)
> 1. **Safety & authenticity** — genuine unit, required certification, battery/charger
>    safety, Indian warranty (not a grey import).
> 2. **Fit** to the elicited use-case.
> 3. **Performance** — judged by **independent benchmarks / measured tests**, not
>    spec-sheet or marketing numbers.
> 4. **Reliability & support** — build, warranty, **service network**, **software /
>    OS-update longevity**, repairability / spares.
> 5. **Usability & value.**
>
> ## Where to fetch (durable addresses; their contents change — verify)
> - **Independent measured tests** (weight these heavily; electronics has strong ones):
>   e.g. **rtings** (TVs, monitors, headphones, earbuds), **Notebookcheck** (laptops —
>   thermals + battery), **GSMArena** (phone specs), **DC Rainmaker** (wearable GPS/HR
>   accuracy), camera testing sites — plus reviewer consensus. Use maker pages for raw
>   specs only.
> - **India certification & energy (verify status live):** **BIS CRS** (Compulsory
>   Registration Scheme — many electronics must be registered; check if this item is
>   *currently* covered and that the registration is genuine), **BEE star rating**
>   (energy — mandatory for several appliances; ratings are periodically re-baselined,
>   so verify the *current* rating), battery/charger safety standards, e-waste / RoHS.
>   Always "check whether this applies now," never asserted.
>
> **Evidence hierarchy:** the elicited use-case → independent measured tests /
> benchmarks → reliability & support track record → maker specs → user reviews **for
> real-world/usability signals** (electronics reviews matter more than in food, but
> still weight measured tests over star ratings, and watch for fake/incentivised
> reviews and counterfeits).
>
> **Classify & authenticity:** confirm it's the exact India model/variant; check
> **grey-import vs Indian-warranty** (missing bands, no service, voided warranty) and
> **refurbished-sold-as-new**. See *Exact product identity* in `instructions.md`.

**Schema per sub-category:** Decision questions (elicit first) → What actually
matters (axes) → Marketing traps (durable) → Where to fetch → Safety / cert leads.
**Generate candidates from the criteria + current market, not a remembered
flagship.** Then run the flow in `compare-products.md` (shortlist + clear #1 + price
last). **To add a category, copy this structure into `category-<name>.md`.**

## 1. Smartphones
- **Decision questions** — budget? **OS / ecosystem** (Android vs iPhone; what you
  already own)? primary use (**camera / gaming / battery / all-day basic**)? size
  preference? how long you'll keep it (update longevity)?
- **What matters** — chipset performance tier; RAM; **display** (panel, refresh,
  real brightness); **camera in real-world tests** (not MP count); **battery +
  charging** (measured); build / IP rating; **software-update commitment** (years of
  OS + security updates); India **5G band** support; storage.
- **Marketing traps** — **megapixel theatre** ("108MP" — sensor size + processing
  matter more); "AI camera"; "up to Xhr battery"; refresh-rate marketing without
  panel quality; **grey imports** (no Indian warranty, missing bands); refurbished
  sold as new.
- **Where to fetch** — GSMArena (specs), independent camera/battery tests, DXOMARK
  (with caution), display tests, the maker's stated update policy.
- **Safety / cert leads** — BIS CRS (phones are registered — verify); genuine
  charger/battery; Indian warranty + service.

## 2. Laptops & tablets
- **Decision questions** — budget? **OS / ecosystem** (Windows / macOS / ChromeOS /
  iPad)? **primary use** (office & browsing / coding & dev / gaming / creative photo
  & video / student)? **portability vs power**? screen needs? keep-for-how-long?
- **What matters** — CPU/GPU tier **for that use**; RAM (soldered / upgradable?);
  storage (upgradable?); **display** (panel, resolution, brightness, colour gamut for
  creative); **battery life in tests**; **thermals / sustained performance** (not
  just the chip name); build; ports; keyboard & trackpad; repairability; warranty &
  service; support life.
- **Marketing traps** — **the flagship-as-default trap** (a MacBook Pro is *not*
  "best" for a budget, gaming, or Windows/Linux-software user — match to the use);
  CPU names without thermal context (**throttling**); "gaming" branding vs measured
  sustained performance; "up to Xhr battery" vs tested; 2-in-1 hype; grey imports.
- **Where to fetch** — Notebookcheck (deep reviews, thermals, battery), reviewer
  benchmarks, maker specs; for tablets, check **app/ecosystem fit** for the real task.
- **Safety / cert leads** — BIS CRS (laptops/tablets registered — verify); charger
  safety; Indian warranty & service.

## 3. Audio (headphones / earbuds / Bluetooth speakers)
- **Decision questions** — **type** (over-ear / in-ear / true-wireless / speaker)?
  **use** (commute / gym / calls / home listening / outdoor / party)? **ANC** needed?
  wired or wireless? portability? budget? **sound preference** (neutral vs extra
  bass)? **water/IP** needed? phone OS (affects codec)?
- **What matters** — **measured sound quality + signature**; **ANC quality**
  (measured, not claimed); battery (+ case); comfort/fit; **mic quality** for calls;
  **codec** (AAC / aptX / LDAC vs your phone); IP rating; multipoint; latency
  (gaming). *Speakers:* real output + distortion at volume, bass, battery, IP, pairing.
- **Marketing traps** — **PMPO / "peak" watts** (meaningless — look for RMS / measured
  output); "Hi-Res" badge without real support; "50hr battery" (case-inclusive);
  unmeasured "deep bass" / ANC claims; **counterfeits of popular earbuds** (rampant on
  marketplaces).
- **Where to fetch** — rtings (measured), reviewer measurements, maker specs;
  authenticity check for popular models.
- **Safety / cert leads** — BIS (some audio under CRS — verify); battery safety;
  Indian warranty; a **hearing-safety** note for high-volume / kids' use.

## 4. TVs & monitors
- **Decision questions (TV)** — room size + **viewing distance → size**; budget; use
  (**movies/HDR / sports / console gaming / bright room**); panel preference?
- **What matters (TV)** — **panel tech** (OLED vs Mini-LED vs QLED/LED); **real
  brightness (nits) + contrast / local dimming**; **actual HDR performance**; motion /
  **120 Hz + HDMI 2.1** for gaming; **input lag** (gaming); upscaling; smart-OS + app
  support + ad experience; built-in sound is usually weak (plan a soundbar).
- **Marketing traps (TV)** — **fake / entry "HDR"** (needs real brightness + dimming
  to mean anything); "4K" as the only selling point; **inflated contrast ratios**;
  **motion-interpolation "Hz"** vs true refresh; PMPO speaker watts; a "gaming TV"
  lacking HDMI 2.1.
- **Decision questions (monitor)** — use (**office / creative-colour / gaming /
  coding**)? size + resolution? budget?
- **What matters (monitor)** — panel (IPS / VA / OLED); resolution + ppi; **refresh +
  response** (gaming); **colour accuracy + gamut** for creative (factory calibration,
  % sRGB / DCI-P3); brightness / real HDR; ergonomics/stand; ports (USB-C PD).
- **Marketing traps (monitor)** — **"1 ms"** (GtG vs MPRT); **"HDR400" ≈ not real
  HDR**; high refresh on a slow panel; gamut claims with no calibration data.
- **Where to fetch** — rtings (TVs + monitors, measured), reviewer tests, maker specs.
- **Safety / cert leads** — BIS CRS (TVs/monitors — verify); **BEE rating for TVs**
  (energy — verify current); warranty & service.

## 5. Wearables (smartwatch / fitness band)
- **Decision questions** — **phone OS** (Apple Watch needs iPhone; Wear OS / others
  for Android)? use (**notifications / fitness / health metrics / sports-GPS /
  simple band**)? budget? **battery-life** priority? standalone LTE?
- **What matters** — **phone compatibility** (a hard gate); **sensor accuracy**
  (HR / SpO2 / GPS — varies a lot; treat readings as indicative); battery; build /
  water-resistance; app ecosystem; display.
- **Marketing traps** — **"medical-grade" health claims** (most are *wellness*, not
  diagnostic; some BP/ECG features are unvalidated); "14-day battery" (with features
  off); overstated GPS / HR accuracy.
- **Safety / health note** — **wearable health readings are not a substitute for a
  medical device or clinical measurement.** If someone is relying on them for a
  condition, say so and point to a validated device / clinician (ties to the
  medical-device boundary — a future `category-health-devices.md`).
- **Where to fetch** — DC Rainmaker (sports GPS/HR accuracy), rtings, reviewer tests,
  maker specs.
- **Safety / cert leads** — BIS (verify); battery; warranty.

## 6. Power banks & chargers (safety-heavy)
- **Decision questions** — **capacity need** (your device battery × how many charges)?
  **fast-charging standard** your device uses (PD / PPS / QC)? portability (note the
  **airline ~100 Wh limit**)? number of ports? budget?
- **What matters** — **real usable capacity** (rated mAh ≠ delivered output — expect
  ~60–70% at 5 V); **output wattage + matching protocol**; **safety protections**
  (over-current / temperature); weight/size; build; **brand trust** (battery safety
  is critical).
- **Marketing traps** — **inflated mAh** (cell rating vs usable output — the classic);
  "fast charge" without the protocol your device needs; **no-name cells** (fire risk);
  "GaN" as a buzzword; counterfeit chargers.
- **Where to fetch** — independent capacity/output tests, teardown reviews, maker specs.
- **Safety / cert leads** — **BIS CRS applies to power banks (verify)**; genuine cell +
  protections; airline capacity labelling; **avoid uncertified / no-name (real fire
  hazard)** — a hard safety flag, not a preference.

## 7. Large home appliances (fridge, AC, washing machine, microwave)
- **Decision questions** — **capacity/size** for the household + space? budget
  **including running cost**? use specifics (**AC:** room size → tonnage; **fridge:**
  family size → litres + type; **washer:** load + front/top; **microwave:** solo /
  grill / convection)? inverter or not?
- **What matters** — **BEE star rating** (energy — big running-cost impact, especially
  AC & fridge); **capacity fit** (don't over-buy); **inverter tech** (efficiency +
  noise); core performance (cooling / wash quality / even heating); **reliability +
  service network** (critical at this size); warranty (compressor/motor); noise;
  features that matter vs gimmicks.
- **Marketing traps** — over-sizing tonnage/capacity; **star-rating confusion**
  (ratings are periodically re-baselined — an old "5-star" ≠ today's; **verify the
  current rating**); "digital inverter" marketing; smart features as gimmicks;
  ignoring installation/service.
- **Where to fetch** — the **BEE label** (verify current), reviewer + long-term
  reliability reports, service-network reputation, maker specs.
- **Safety / cert leads** — **BEE mandatory for several (verify which + the current
  rating)**; BIS / electrical safety; installation + service; AC refrigerant type.
- **Cross-ref** — for microwave-safe **containers / food-contact**, see
  `category-kitchen.md`.

## 8. Kitchen & small home electricals (mixer, kettle, air-fryer, toaster, induction…)
- **Decision questions** — use + frequency? capacity / wattage need? counter space?
  budget?
- **What matters** — **motor power adequacy + durability** (mixers/grinders); build;
  **food-contact parts** (food-grade, BPA-free — see kitchen file); safety cut-offs;
  ease of cleaning; warranty & service; noise.
- **Marketing traps** — **inflated wattage** claims; "X-in-one" gimmicks; non-food-grade
  jars; no service / spares.
- **Where to fetch** — reviewer durability tests, maker specs.
- **Safety / cert leads** — **ISI / BIS is mandatory for many small electricals —
  verify which apply now**; electrical safety; warranty & service.
- **Cross-ref** — this covers the **appliance-quality / electrical** lens; for the
  **food-contact-material** lens (jar/coating safety, BPA), use `category-kitchen.md`
  §8. The two are complementary.
