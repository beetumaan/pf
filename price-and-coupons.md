# Price & coupons — exact SKU, genuine final price (any category)

**Use when** I've settled on **one product** (or a compare shortlist, when I gave a
budget) and ask its price / where to buy it. Goal: a clear **buy path** — the best
*genuine* deal and the site to get it from. **Category-agnostic.** Runs **only when
price is in play**, and **never changes a quality verdict** — "good for me" is
decided price-blind first; a low price can't launder a weak product.

Use my standing **location / pincode and any card / app preferences from
`instructions.md`** (or a different one if I specify for this query).

Run in order:

### 1. Lock the exact identity
Same **brand, exact name, variant / formula, size, pack count, and India-vs-import
SKU**. **Never merge listings** with a different size, pack count, variant,
formulation, sample / refill, free gift, subscription, or imported version — price
them separately or normalise (step 3).

### 2. Collect listings
Across the trusted Indian retailers relevant to the category (Amazon.in, Flipkart,
Nykaa / Myntra / Ajio, Croma / Vijay Sales, Blinkit / Zepto / Instamart) plus a
**price-history / comparison aggregator** (MySmartPrice, Smartprix, Buyhatke) to
sanity-check a fake "discount" off an inflated MRP. For each listing record:
retailer + **marketplace seller**, exact-SKU match, **stock / deliverability to my
pincode**, item price, **mandatory shipping**, immediate public discount,
conditional offers, return policy (where authenticity matters), and the **check
time in IST**.

### 3. Normalise the price
- **Unconditional final price** = item price + mandatory shipping − discount any
  buyer gets immediately (no code, no login).
- **Unit price** = unconditional final price ÷ grams / ml / uses — so a small pack
  can't masquerade as cheaper.
- Keep **conditional** offers *separate*, each labelled: bank-card, membership,
  subscription, wallet, first-order coupon, delayed / capped cashback, bundle
  freebie. **Never present a maximum theoretical discount as the normal price.**

### 4. Trust screen (a higher trusted price can beat a lower suspicious one)
Exclude or clearly flag: wrong / mismatched SKU; unclear or suspicious seller;
grey-market / import listing without a compliant label + importer details;
near-expiry / open-box (unless I accept it); out-of-stock or non-deliverable;
implausible price with weak seller evidence; a price that needs an undisclosed
login or coupon that can't be verified.

### 5. Rank → buy path
1. **Lowest genuine unconditional final price** (the honest "you'll pay this").
2. **Lowest conditional price**, with the exact condition stated.
3. **Best authorised / lowest-risk source** (for authenticity-sensitive items).
4. **Unit-price comparison** when pack sizes differ.
I have no preferred retailer — rank purely on the best genuine deal wherever it's
in stock. Every code is **"candidate — verify at checkout,"** never a promise; I
surface offers, I don't apply them or transact — that's mine.

### 6. Output
A short table:

| Retailer / seller | Exact SKU | Stock / pincode | Item ₹ | Shipping | Immediate discount | Final ₹ | Unit ₹ | Conditional offers | Trust notes | Checked (IST) |

Close with **one direct buy-path steer** ("buy from X at ₹Y; likely pay ≈ ₹Z, or
₹W if a candidate code works"), a **price-confidence** line, and the **`Sources
used:`** tally by type (retailer / aggregator / affiliate coupon site).

---
**Honest limits (read this):** prices and *especially* coupons are the **least
reliable** data to fetch — retail pages are often bot-protected or stale,
quick-commerce is pincode-specific, and most public codes are expired, fake, or
tied to a specific card / app. Everything here is a **snapshot to verify at
checkout**, and I never claim a retailer is "always cheapest" — only what I saw at
the recorded time. This is exactly why price sits *outside* the quality verdict.
