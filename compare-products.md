# Compare: a few products, strong vs weak

**Use when** I want help **choosing** — a category, or a shortlist I name. (One
product I've already picked → `deep-dive.md`.) Runs **Quick or Deep**.
**Price-blind by default** — price is a separate step (`price-and-coupons.md`).
**But if I give a budget or ask for "best value / cheapest",** bring price in:
filter candidates to my budget, still rank by **quality within** it (a low price
never overrides safety or quality), and add the price winners in step 6.

Run in order:

### 1. Pin the need + constraints
What does "good **for me**" mean here: the job to be done (e.g. sensitive teeth,
oily scalp, acne-prone skin), plus hard constraints — allergies / irritants,
dietary / vegan / formulation needs, and a rough **tier** (basic / mid / premium —
a *tier*, not a price lookup). If unstated, ask one tight question or state the
assumption you're using.
**Run the *Safety* gate in `instructions.md` first** — flag who should skip an
option and offer the safe alternative; escalate genuine symptoms before any
shortlist.

### 2. Set the bar (standards-first)
Define what separates good from bad — from the matching **`category-*.md`** if it
exists, else classify-first + **EU → FDA → India (CDSCO / BIS)** per
`instructions.md`, and say so. **Set the bar at EU level** for cosmetic safety; a
candidate clearing only India's legal floor (CDSCO / BIS) is a flag, not a pass —
but don't let an EU *cosmetic* ban veto an effective *drug*-class active. **Deep**
pulls the *current* limits live (CosIng / EUR-Lex / SCCS); **Quick / list**
screening leans on the outline — flag any figure as unverified. Rank by the
**priority order** in `instructions.md` (safety → fit → evidence → preferences →
price), not fixed weights.

### 3. Assemble candidates
Find products **available in India** that fit the need and could clear the bar.
**Generate from the criteria, not from brand lists** — the category file's brands
are a cross-check, not a starting point; don't anchor on familiar / advertised
names. For each sourcing result, tag provenance (**affiliate / commission** —
amzn.to, "buy now" CTAs, coupon codes; **brand-owned**; **retailer**;
**independent** — dermatology sites, review aggregators, Reddit / forums); lean on
independent, and **if affiliate content informs a pick, say so in the output**.
Quick: 3–4 contenders. Deep: cast wider, including quieter picks experts rate.

### 4. Screen each candidate
Note the **product class** (cosmetic / drug / mixed) and judge by the right regime.
Check each against the bar and apply the `deep-dive.md` ingredient + claim scrutiny
(lightweight in Quick, full in Deep). **Don't infer SPF / UVA / efficacy from the
ingredient list** — prefer product-level tests; mark undisclosed decisive figures
**"not publicly verifiable."** **India-formulation caveat:** the Indian SKU can
differ from the tested / reviewed version — check it matches, and flag authenticity
risk for premium items on marketplaces. **Lock the exact variant / size /
India-vs-import SKU before the final comparison** — never compare a 30 g against a
50 g (see *Exact product identity* in `instructions.md`).

### 5. Evidence pass
- **Reviews:** synthesise a *sample* across sites + YouTube / Reddit / Indian
  forums; weight verified-purchase, detailed, and critical reviews over 5-star
  one-liners. **Report only what you can actually inspect** — many retailers don't
  expose verified-purchase status or review dates, and reused-photo checks aren't
  reliable at scale, so flag a manipulation signal only where it's genuinely
  visible and never claim an audit you couldn't run.
- **Independent data:** third-party tests, expert / dermatology consensus,
  regulatory actions. Say plainly when none exists (that lowers confidence, not
  necessarily rank).

### 6. Present the shortlist — strong vs weak, then the winners
Give a few options (usually **2–3**). For each:
- **Strong points** — where it wins, who it's best for.
- **Weak points** — what you give up, any flags.
- the **one decisive point**.
Then name the winners (often different products):
- **Best overall** — strongest quality + fit. *Always given — the price-blind pick.*
- **Best value** and **Lowest genuine price** — *only when price is in play* (I
  gave a budget, or asked "best value / cheapest"). Run `price-and-coupons.md` for
  the shortlisted SKUs, then rank **best value** (quality per genuine final price)
  and **lowest genuine price** (cheapest trustworthy listing for the same exact
  SKU). Otherwise skip these and keep it price-blind.

### 7. What it means for me
The decision in a line or two. Separate **hard facts** (meets / fails a bar) from
**preference calls** (scent, texture, taste, cost) that are mine to make.

**Then close with the `Sources used:` line** (tally by type — see
`instructions.md`), so it's obvious how much independent evidence backs the
shortlist.

---
**Honest limits:** reviews are a **sample**, not a census; new / niche products
have thin independent data; Indian formulation & counterfeit caveats apply; verify
current limits each time. "Best for me" is part evidence, part preference — I'll
nail the evidence and be explicit about where the choice is yours.
