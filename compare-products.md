# Compare: a few products, strong vs weak

**Use when** I want help **choosing** — a category, or a shortlist I name. (One
product I've already picked → `deep-dive.md`.) Runs **Quick or Deep**.
**No pricing — I check that myself.**

Run in order:

### 1. Pin the need + constraints
What does "good **for me**" mean here: the job to be done (e.g. sensitive teeth,
oily scalp, acne-prone skin), plus hard constraints — allergies / irritants,
dietary / vegan / formulation needs, and a rough **tier** (basic / mid / premium —
a *tier*, not a price lookup). If unstated, ask one tight question or state the
assumption you're using.

### 2. Set the bar (standards-first)
Define what separates good from bad — from the matching **`category-*.md`** if it
exists, else from **EU → FDA → India** per `instructions.md`, and say so. **Set
the bar at EU level;** a candidate clearing only Indian (FSSAI / BIS) minimums is a
flag, not a pass. **Deep** pulls the *current* limits live (CosIng / EUR-Lex /
SCCS); **Quick / list** screening leans on the outline — flag any figure as
unverified.

### 3. Assemble candidates
Find products **available in India** that fit the need and could clear the bar.
Prefer **genuinely well-regarded** over **heavily advertised**. Quick: 3–4 obvious
contenders. Deep: cast wider, including quieter picks experts rate.

### 4. Screen each candidate
Check each against the bar and apply the `deep-dive.md` ingredient + claim
scrutiny (lightweight in Quick, full in Deep). **India-formulation caveat:** the
Indian SKU can differ from the tested / reviewed version — check it matches, and
flag authenticity risk for premium items on marketplaces.

### 5. Evidence pass
- **Reviews:** synthesise a *sample* across sites + YouTube / Reddit / Indian
  forums; weight verified-purchase, detailed, and critical reviews over 5-star
  one-liners; call out manipulated ratings.
- **Independent data:** third-party tests, expert / dermatology consensus,
  regulatory actions. Say plainly when none exists (that lowers confidence, not
  necessarily rank).

### 6. Present the shortlist — strong vs weak
Give a few options (usually **2–3**). For each:
- **Strong points** — where it wins, who it's best for.
- **Weak points** — what you give up, any flags.
- the **one decisive point**.
Close with an **"if I had to pick" steer.** No price ranking — note only that price
is mine to weigh.

### 7. What it means for me
The decision in a line or two. Separate **hard facts** (meets / fails a bar) from
**preference calls** (scent, texture, taste, cost) that are mine to make.

---
**Honest limits:** reviews are a **sample**, not a census; new / niche products
have thin independent data; Indian formulation & counterfeit caveats apply; verify
current limits each time. "Best for me" is part evidence, part preference — I'll
nail the evidence and be explicit about where the choice is yours.
